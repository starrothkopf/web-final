TO MAKE A DADAIST POEM

Take a newspaper.

Take some scissors.

Choose from this paper an article of the length you want to make your poem.

Cut out the article.

Next carefully cut out each of the words that makes up this article and put them all in a bag.

Shake gently.

Next take out each cutting one after the other.

The poem will resemble you.

—Tristan Tzara, 1920

Writers and artists from many eras have used randomness to resist to traditional methods of meaning-making, to uncover the unconscious, or to reject the value of utility all together. But to a computer scientist, randomness is an essential tool. Unpredictable algorithms allow statisticians to sample without bias, security specialists to encrypt, and game designers to make roguelikes replayable.


This website is an art object. It offers examples of randomness as a site of unity between the creative and the technical, using uselessness to release the algorithm from practicality and into the functions of lyric and myth.
